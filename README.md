# coffee-sale-analysis
The main goal of this project is to analyze sales data and provide quick insights into business performance. It focuses on finding key trends and improving decision-making through visual analytics.This project presents an interactive Coffee Sales Analysis Dashboard built in Microsoft Excel. The aim of this analysis is to understand sales performance, customer behavior, and regional trends across coffee products.The dashboard consolidates raw data from multiple sheets — including customer details, order transactions, and product information — into a visually engaging and data-driven report. It helps answer key business questions such as: Who are the top-performing customers? Which coffee products generate the most revenue? What are the sales trends across different countries? How does customer distribution vary geographically? Through this project, Excel has been utilized not just as a spreadsheet tool, but as a powerful analytical platform for business intelligence and decision-making.

DATASET OVERVIEW

The Excel file includes several sheets such as: Customers – Details of each customer. Orders – Information about every coffee order. Products – List of coffee types and prices. Total Sales – Overall sales summary. Top 10 Customers – Highest revenue-generating customers. TS-Country – Total sales by country. No. of Customers per Country – Count of customers in each region. Dashboard – The main visual report

DASHBOARD FEATURES

The dashboard shows total sales, number of customers, and country-wise performance. It includes interactive charts and slicers to filter data by product, date, or country. It highlights top-performing products and customers using pivot charts and summary tables

INSIGHTS

The United States leads in coffee sales. Arabica is the most popular coffee type. The top 10 customers account for a large share of total revenue. Customer base varies by region and product type.

HOW TO USE

Download or clone this repository. Open Coffee Order analysis.xlsx in Microsoft Excel (version 2016 or newer recommended). Navigate to the Dashboard sheet. Use slicers and filters to interact with the charts .
